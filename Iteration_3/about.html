An Apple A Day was created to address potentially preventable hospitalisation (PPH) in Australia

One in every 15 hospitalisations in Australia are classified as potentially preventable, of which chronic conditions is a key contributor.


However, these chronic conditions can largely be mitigated with timely, effective and appropriate intervention. Yet there is little community-based information and awareness about how and why one can and should take charge of their risk factors and lifestyle at an early stage to ensure a healthy future.


To address this problem, our team is tackling the inherent difficulties and language barriers to the delivery of health services and information to people from culturally and linguistically diverse (CALD) backgrounds.

在澳大利亚，每15例住院中就有1例被认为是可预防的，其中慢性病是关键因素。


然而，这些慢性病可以通过及时，有效且适当的干预措施来减缓症状。但很少有基于群体的信息普及和认知去告诉大家如何在早期去防范会导致这些病情的因素和原因以及如何管理他们的生活方式，来确保有一个健康的未来。


为解决此问题，我们的团队向来自于不同文化和语言背景的人群提供医疗服务和信息以此来解决他们的困难和语言障碍。



我们的主要目标是:
  • Lower the instances of hospitalisation stemming from chronic conditions, ultimately leading to a reduction in PPH levels.
  • Address the inherent difficulties and language barriers to the delivery of health services and information to people from CALD backgrounds.
  • Promote equity and access to information surrounding preventable chronic conditions.
  • Empower CALD Australians to understand chronic conditions, consider lifestyle interventions, and to seek medical advice.
  • 降低因慢性病而住院的情况，最终致使潜在的可预防性疫病的病例降低。
  • 解决从来自于不同文化和语言背景的人群的固有困难和语言障碍，并向他们提供医疗服务和信息。
  • 促进和获取有关于可预防的慢性病的信息。
  • 让来自于不同文化和语言背景的澳大利亚人了解慢性病，并考虑改善生活方式和寻求医疗建议。
