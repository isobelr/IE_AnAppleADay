Healthcare Explainer

The Australian healthcare system at a glance

The public system

The public health system is owned and governed by state and territory governments and the services provided are funded by local, state and federal governments. It is made up of number of components, including public hospitals, community-based services, and affiliated health organisations. As an Australian, you can access care within the public health system for free or at a lower cost through Medicare.

The private system

The private system is owned and managed privately through health service providers such as private hospitals, specialist medical and allied health, and pharmacies. The services you can access through the private system are funded by a combination of government and private entities, including: private health insurance premiums (paid by customers), people paying directly for private treatment, and private health organisations.
