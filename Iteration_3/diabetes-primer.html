Type 2 Diabetes Primer

Here's what you need to know about type 2 diabetes.

Symptoms

The symptoms of type 2 diabetes often develop slowly and can be hard to recognise. In fact, many people have type 2 diabetes for years and don't know it. However, there are some key symptoms:

  • Excessive thirst
  • Frequent urination
  • Increased hunger
  • Fatigue
  • Blurry vision
  • Slow-healing sores
  • Frequent infections
  • Areas of darkened skin

What's the most common symptom of type 2 diabetes?

Surprisingly, nothing! That's right, most people don't experience any particular symptoms. This is why it's so important to keep on top of your risk factors, and to get checked by a doctor if you are at a high risk of develpoing type 2 diabetes.

Risk Factors

  • Age
    The risk of type 2 diabetes increases as you get older, especially if you are aged 45 years or older.
  • Family history
    The risk of type 2 diabetes increases if you have a family history of diabetes
  • Race or ethnicity
    Although it's unclear why, certain people — including those from a Chinese background — are at higher risk.
  • Inactivity
    The less active you are, the greater your risk of type 2 diabetes. Physical activity helps you control your weight, uses up glucose as energy and makes your cells more sensitive to insulin.
  • Weight
    Being overweight is a main risk factor for type 2 diabetes. However, you don't have to be overweight to develop type 2 diabetes.
